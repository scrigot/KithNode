## 2. Value comments for r/FinancialCareers (no product mention)

**"No connections, non-target, how do I break in":**
> The lever that changed it for me was sorting who I reached out to by connection strength instead of seniority. A second-year analyst who shares your school or club replies way more than a cold MD. Build your list as: same school + same club/team first, then same school, then same firm path, then everyone else. Reach out to the warm ones first while you're still figuring out your pitch.

**"My cold emails get no replies":**
> Two things did more than anything else. One, put the actual shared thing in the subject line (same school, same hometown), not "quick question." Two, send the follow-up. Most people send one email and quit, but a single follow-up around day 5-7 roughly doubled my response rate. The reply is usually sitting in the second email, not the first.

**"How long before I follow up / is it annoying":**
> Day 5 to 7 on the first follow-up, cap it at two or three touches total. After that you're not persistent, you're a problem. One short, specific follow-up that references your first message is fine. People are busy, not ignoring you on purpose.

**"Is it weird to message alumni I don't know":**
> Not if you lead with the shared thing and ask for their path, not a job. "I'm at [school] too, saw you went from [group] to [firm], would love to hear how you thought about that move" gets a reply. "Can I pick your brain / any openings" does not. Ask about them, not what they can do for you.
